Rails Girls Summer of Code

Core organizer


Rails Girls Summer of Code is a 3 months global scholarship that sponsors women to contribute full-time to Open Source Projects with the help of coaches and mentors. Since 2013, RGSoC has trained 145 students, raised almost 450.000 USD and created a community of over 800 students, coaches, mentors, supervisors and organizers.

On this program I was one of the 4 core organizers. My main areas of responsibility were marketing, pr, student's support and sponsors management.


Marketing and Design

Looking at the RGSoC's stats on facebook, twitter and mailchimp, I realised that, just like any other organization, the team didn't have enough time to focus on communication and creating content. However, RGSoC is backed up by an incredible community and this is the dream of any brand: to have a community that believes in you and in what you do.

My main goals were: connecting even more the people who are parte of the organization with the people that are part of RGSoC in many ways; make sure that the content was easily associated with the brand, so that it would be more easily recognizable; creating content that was both authentic and fun, making people feel excited about the posts and consequently shared them; grow the number of followers organically and a strong foundation to reach even more people the following years.

Regarding Twitter, I am happy to say that in less than a year, the RGSoC's account increased their impressions by 400%, and in May 2016, with the Thank You Board Campaign, the reach broke all records, having had 37 times the impressions hey had in May 2015. Overall the number of followeres was up around 150% and the number of tweets 163%.
