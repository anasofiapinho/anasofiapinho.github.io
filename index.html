Olá and welcome! My name is

ANA SOFIA PINHO

I am a Digital Marketeer, Multimedia Designer
and Project Manager based in Portugal.

Also, a diversity activist in the making.



Around ten years ago, I was graduating in Industrial Engineering and working at Bosch. My projects were focused on improvement of processes and Lean Manufacturing — not Lean Startup, that came later! I loved what I did, but soon I felt that I had to let the creative side come out and change careers.

Slowly I started designing logos and websites for the companies I worked for as a Lean Consultant. After participating in a couple of Startup Weekends in 2011 I finally felt at home and everything changed. I started working with startups while at the same time I organized tech events.

A year later I organized the first programming workshop for women, Rails Girls Porto 2013. I had no idea how important this would be for me. Since then I have been promoting diversity initiatives among many organizations, such as Geekettes and RGSoC.

To organize all of these events and managing a community online and offline, I ended up focusing more on social media marketing and creation of content. I really enjoyed interacting with the community and crafting content and this is how I ended up in marketing.

In the past four years I have been working with small startups and international companies as a social media strategist and content creator. I still continued to work on projects related to operations and more recently I'm having a blast working on customer support.

Yes, in case you were wondering, I am a polymath.

WORK

Work is love. For me, it's incredibly motivating to think about how we as human beings can make this world a better place through our work. And that's love.

It doesn't matter if I am making a marketing strategy, designing a gif, replying to emails or writing HTML, the polymath in me will look at each task from different perspectives, — as an engineer, manager, operations worker, designer, marketeer, user and customer, — in order to bring as much value as I can.

Currently I am looking for a job in social media, customer support or operations.

DIVERSITY

I remember being at a coffee shop in Lisbon with a friend when he talked about Rails Girls and programming workshops for women*. Long story short, I knew I had to bring those workshops to Portugal and in March of 2013, the first Rails Girls workshop ever organized in this country happened in Porto.

Rails Girls Porto 2013 Friday Night Hug; Moderating the Global Geekettes Meetup Panel with Ida Tin (Clue), Leslie Miley (Slack), Hollie Haggans (Digital Ocean), Lu Li (Blooming Founders) and Filipa Larangeira (Orangeboom); Friday Afternoon Hug with RGSoC's team and alumna; Geekettes interview for National Portuguese TV station RTP1.

There are no words to describe the happiness and excitement when we gather women in events around science and technology. I feel privileged to be able to welcome women and other minorities to tech, a place that has always been theirs, but for many many reasons it might seem like they don't belong there.

The United Nations estimates that we need 170 years to reach "gender parity". We really can't wait that long because just like Neil deGrasse Tyson says, "Science and Tecnology are the engines of the economy of the future", and last time I checked, technology is evolving exponentially, so we need to get going if we want to build the future society of the future that is both diverse and inclusive for all of its citizens.

Besides all of my work with other organizations, I am available for writing articles and giving talks about this topic. For speaking enquiries or any other questions, please send an email to me@anasofiapinho.com.
